epilogos is a system for the visualization and analysis of large numbers of chromatin state models.
It scales up easily to hundreds of epigenomes, provides an intuitive overview of genomic regions of interest,
and allows for precise modeling and inference of epigenomic data sets.

The current page is only a preview, as we're working on further applications of epilogos,
including, but not limited to, comparative epigenomics and prototype discovery and searching.

UPDATE (2015/03/18):
As part of the Roadmap Epigenomics Project, epilogos are now available for all Roadmap chromatin state models,
at the web portal of the Roadmap project. Thanks to Anshul Kundaje and Jin Wook Lee at Stanford for facilitating this.
